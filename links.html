General Information for Voters
USA.gov Voting and Elections Guide

This is the US government's official guide to voting and elections. Find answers to common questions about voting in the United States.

Election Assistance Commission

Among other things, the EAC serves as a national archive and information resource for election administration. Their website offers a wealth of practical resources for voters, as well as guidelines for election administration procedures.
Vote 411

Vote411 was created by the League of Women Voters, and provides general and state-specific nonpartisan information to the public on much of the election process.
Federal Elections Commission

The Federal Elections Commission is an independent regulatory agency that administers and enforces federal campaign finance laws. Their website allows users to learn about these laws, and to see campaign finance disclosures.
The White House

This is the official homepage of the White House, and by extension the Executive Branch. Users can find out more about the current administration, as well as past administrations.

The US Senate

The homepage of the US Senate. Go here to look up information on any acting Senator, learn about active legislation, watch a live floor webcast, and more.

The US House of Representatives

This is the hompage of the US House of Representatives. Users can find out information about current Representatives, learn about legislation, and watch live webcasts of the House in session.

The US Supreme Court

Homepage of the US Supreme Court. Go here to learn about the Court and the Justices, read past opinions, and view the upcoming schedule.

Activism Around the Web
The 65

The 65 is committed to making Congress work for us by mobilizing with a shared common mission, called a Weekly Call to Action. Users can find out what issues are currently being persued, check out calling/writing scripts, and retrieve contact information for their Senators.
Indivisible Guide

Indivisible tries to demystify congressional advocacy by providing a practical organizational guide for local citizen groups. Indivisible groups have been responsible for organizing many of the town halls that have been in the news this February.
Knock Every Door

Knock on Every Door is an attempt to build a largescale, grassroots, volunteer led effort to reach out to potential first time voters. Users can sign up to receive more information on volunteering with local groups.
Run For Something

Run for Something advocates for, and supports, young people (those 35 and under) running for elected office.
314 Action

The goal of 314 Action is to strengthen communication between the STEM community, the public, and elected officials. They hope to acheive this goal in part by advocating for STEM professionals to run for office.
Resistbot

Resistbot is a text message-based bot that makes it easy to send letters/faxes to local representatives. Users can either text "RESIST" to the number 50409, or message the bot over Facebook. After a series of questions, the bot sends the user's personalized message to the local rep's office.
